# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single contemporary workshop Andean duct flute presented as a finished physical object: a slender straight end-blown fipple flute roughly the length of a forearm, with a clean cylindrical body and a slightly wider head end. The defining feature is the fipple voicing assembly near the top — a removable block plugging the head, a flat windway face, and a rectangular sound window cut just below the head datum with a crisp angled labium edge ramped into the bore. A row of round front finger holes runs down the body toward the open foot. Render two believable material readings implied by the packet: a smooth off-white PVC proof-of-tune body with a faintly matte plastic sheen, OR a straight-grain hardwood version (warm cherry/maple/walnut) with a fine satin finish and a contrasting block insert. Realistic small-instrument scale, held-in-two-hands proportions. Clean neutral light-gray studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels, no maker marks.

## In-Context Prompt

The same Andean duct flute resting on a maker's workbench in a quiet contemporary woodworking and acoustics shop, staged mid-build: a length of nominal half-inch PVC stock and a small hardwood blank nearby, a removable cedar or maple block, a chromatic tuner, calipers, a small set of round files, and a hole-layout story stick laid out as if the windway and window are being voiced and tuned by ear. Warm natural window light from the side, shallow depth of field with the fipple window in sharp focus and the tools softly blurred, environmental storytelling of empirical voicing work, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the fipple voicing assembly — the signature mechanism. Frame the head end three-quarters from above so the flat windway face, the thin air channel between block and body, and the rectangular sound window with its crisp angled labium ramp are all clearly readable, edge centered and clean with no torn grain. Show the removable block seated airtight in the bore mouth. Materials and textures: either the matte off-white PVC proof body or warm satin-finished hardwood with a contrasting block insert. Raking light grazing across the labium edge to reveal the ramp and the cut window, very high detail, no measurements, no callout lines.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms, no keys/pads/metal mechanism (this is a simple holes-and-fipple flute); anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
